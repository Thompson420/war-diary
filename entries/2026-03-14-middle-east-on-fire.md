## The War Nobody Asked For

It started on February 28, 2026. Two weeks later, the Middle East is unrecognizable.

On that morning, the United States and Israel launched coordinated strikes on Iran — not a limited "surgical" operation, but a full-scale assault on the Islamic Republic's military infrastructure, nuclear sites, and leadership. Iran's Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei — who had held power since 1989 — was killed in Tehran during the first wave. Senior commanders in the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) perished alongside him.

By March 8, Khamenei's son, Mojtaba Khamenei, was appointed as successor. The regime, shaken but not broken, ordered widespread retaliation.

This is the story of how the war escalated — and why it's already reshaping the geopolitical order.

## Tehran Burns, Gulf States Tremble

The strikes were unprecedented in scale. BBC Verify has confirmed at least 142 separate attacks across Iran since February 28, with approximately 65 strikes hitting Tehran alone. The US military claims it has targeted roughly 6,000 locations across the country.

Nuclear facilities at Natanz were hit. Missile sites were destroyed. Satellite imagery shows damaged aircraft at Mehrabad airport. But the human cost is what matters:

- **1,858+ people killed in Iran** (per Human Rights Activists in Iran)
- **1,286 civilians, including at least 200 children**
- **13 US service personnel killed**
- An Iranian warship sunk in the Indian Ocean — 87 sailors dead

In one incident that shocked the world, a girls' school near an IRGC base in southern Iran was hit on February 28. Iranian authorities say 168 people died — around 110 of them children. BBC Verify, analysing video footage, identified a US Tomahawk missile as the weapon. Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth now faces congressional questions.

Internet access inside Iran has been nearly extinguished. International journalists can't get in. We may never know the full death toll.

## The Gulf Becomes a Battlefield

Iran's response was immediate and desperate — but it didn't stop at Israel.

Missiles and drones began falling across the Gulf:

- **UAE**: 6 dead
- **Kuwait**: 6 dead  
- **Oman, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain**: 2 dead each
- **Iraq**: Struck at Erbil airport; a French soldier killed at a Kurdish military base
- **Turkey**: NATO air defences shot down three Iranian missiles over Turkish airspace
- **Cyprus**: A British military base struck by a drone
- **Dubai**: Hotels — civilian targets — hit by Iranian strikes

Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian later appeared on state TV, apologizing to neighbouring countries. "We did not intend to invade neighbouring countries," he said, announcing that Iranian forces would no longer attack unless attacked first.

The apology came too late.

## Lebanon: The New Front

On March 2, Hezbollah — the Iran-backed militant group in Lebanon — entered the war, firing rockets at Israeli positions to "avenge" Khamenei's death.

Israel responded with devastating force. Beirut's southern suburbs. Nabatieh. The shoreline.

Lebanon's health ministry reports **773 people killed** since March 2, including **103 children**. More than **700,000** have been displaced — around 200,000 of them children.

Israeli ground troops have advanced into southern Lebanon. Two Israeli soldiers have been killed. The war that was supposed to be "limited" now has no end in sight.

## The Political Fallout

In Washington, the war is exposing fractures in the Republican coalition. Trump's most dedicated supporters stand with him — but significant numbers of Republicans have "serious doubts" about the justification for war, according to BBC analysis.

In Israel, Prime Minister Netanyahu frames the bombing campaign as having "transformed the Middle East" in Israel's favour. But pressure is mounting to end the conflict. Hopes of regime change in Iran have faded. The question now is: how does anyone de-escalate?

## What This Means for the Region

This is the most significant Middle East conflict since the Iraq War — and it may be only beginning.

- **Nuclear facilities were hit**: Iran's nuclear program, always contested, is now explicitly a military target
- **The Gulf is no longer safe**: Oil facilities, shipping lanes, and civilian infrastructure are now legitimate targets
- **The US is fully engaged**: Not "supporting" Israel — actively waging war alongside it
- **Regional order is collapsing**: Turkey, a NATO member, shot down Iranian missiles. This is no longer a bilateral conflict.
