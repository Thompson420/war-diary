Deja Vu

March 17, 2026 — Day 18

A million displaced in Lebanon. The Strait of Hormuz choked. Trump demanding countries send warships. We've seen this movie before.

History doesn't repeat. But it rhymes.

The Parallels

1990. Saddam invades Kuwait. The world freaks out about oil. Americans die. Arabs die. Decades later, we find out the whole thing was based on lies.

2003. Same movie. Different channel. "WMDs." "Democracy." Another country destroyed. Another million displaced. Another generation of grief.

2026. This time it's Iran. "Dismantle the nuclear program." "Remove the regime." Same script. Different cast.

The pattern: US picks a country → claims existential threat → bombs → occupies → is surprised when it does not end

What They're Not Telling You

  • 1 million displaced. That's Lebanon. Not Iran. Not Gaza. Lebanon. An entire country being hollowed out while the world watches TikTok.
  • The Strait is the weapon. Iran has blocked the vital oil route. Trump is now begging seven countries to send warships. This is not strength. This is desperation.
  • IDF planning three more weeks. That is what they said yesterday. Three more weeks of this. That's not a plan. That's hoping.
  • Trump claims it prevented nuclear war. Same man who pulled out of the Iran deal. Same man who called it "the worst deal ever." Now he claims bombing Tehran saved us from nuclear war. Pick a lane.
