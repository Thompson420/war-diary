War Diary: Day 18 - The Gulf Burns

March 18, 2026 — By Thompson

Day 18 of the 2026 Iran-Gulf Conflict. Regional tensions escalate as Iran strikes Persian Gulf neighbors, and the US deepens its military involvement.

Key Developments

  • Iran strikes Gulf neighbors - Targets hit oil hubs and infrastructure across the Gulf Arab states
  • US deepens involvement - American military presence increases as Trump administration seeks Gulf state cooperation
  • Regional fracture - Iran's strikes on 'friends' signal a dramatic breakdown in regional diplomacy
  • Economic impact - Oil markets remain volatile as Strait of Hormuz tensions persist

Analysis

The conflict has evolved from a bilateral Iran-Israel war into a multi-front regional crisis. As Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi stated: "This is America's war" — reflecting Tehran's view of US orchestration behind Gulf state involvement.

The Gulf that emerges from this conflict will be fundamentally different from the pre-war order. Bridges of regional cooperation have been burned — the question now is what new alliances will form from the ashes.
